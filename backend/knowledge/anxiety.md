Definitions of generalized anxiety, social anxiety, and phobias. It should detail cognitive symptoms (racing thoughts, catastrophic thinking, future-tripping) and physical symptoms (muscle tension, elevated heart rate, shallow breathing).

How to deal:

Grounding techniques: The 5-4-3-2-1 method (naming 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste) forces the brain out of anxious spirals and back into the present environment.

Cognitive reframing: Identifying irrational "worst-case scenario" thoughts and actively challenging them with concrete evidence.

Tactical breathing: Extending the exhale to be longer than the inhale (e.g., in for 4 seconds, out for 6). This physically signals safety to the parasympathetic nervous system.
