The mechanics of a panic attack—an abrupt, severe misfire of the body's fight-or-flight response. It should list acute physical symptoms like hyperventilation, chest tightness, dizziness, and a feeling of impending doom.

How to deal:

The mammalian dive reflex: Splashing ice-cold water on the face or holding an ice cube. The intense temperature shock immediately forces the heart rate to drop and disrupts the panic cycle.

Box breathing: Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. This regulates oxygen and stops hyperventilation.

Riding the wave: Actively acknowledging that it is a panic attack, remembering that it cannot physically harm you, and accepting that it will naturally peak and subside within 10 to 15 minutes.
