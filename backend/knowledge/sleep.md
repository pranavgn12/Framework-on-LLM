Sleep hygiene principles, circadian rhythms, the architecture of sleep phases (REM vs. Deep sleep), and the physical and cognitive consequences of sleep deprivation and insomnia.

How to deal:

Lock the schedule: Waking up and going to bed at the exact same time every day, even on weekends, to permanently anchor the circadian rhythm.

Temperature control: Keeping the bedroom cool (around 60–67°F or 15–19°C) to facilitate the core body temperature drop required to trigger sleep onset.

Light management: Dimming lights and killing blue-light-emitting screens 60 to 90 minutes before bed, as blue light severely suppresses melatonin production.
