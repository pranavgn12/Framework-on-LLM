Clinical definitions of depressive disorders, highlighting core symptoms like anhedonia (the inability to feel pleasure), chronic lethargy, brain fog, and changes in appetite or sleep patterns.

How to deal:

Behavioral activation: Doing small, manageable tasks (like taking a shower or stepping outside) before the motivation strikes. In a depressed state, action often has to precede motivation.

Micro-routines: Creating a very low-friction daily structure to reduce the cognitive load of decision-making when energy is extremely low.

Professional intervention: Acknowledging when self-help isn't enough and engaging in evidence-based therapies (like Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) or consulting a doctor for medical support.
