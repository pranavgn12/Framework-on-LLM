Attachment styles (secure, anxious, avoidant), communication frameworks, conflict resolution patterns, and boundary setting in romantic, platonic, and familial dynamics.

How to deal:

"I" Statements: Framing conflicts around personal feelings rather than accusations (e.g., "I feel overwhelmed when plans change suddenly," instead of "You are always so flaky").

Active listening: Focusing entirely on understanding the other person's perspective before formulating your own defense or response.

Defining and enforcing boundaries: Clearly communicating what behaviors are acceptable and establishing natural consequences if those boundaries are repeatedly crossed.
