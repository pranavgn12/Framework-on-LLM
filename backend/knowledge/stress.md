The difference between acute stress (short-term, situational) and chronic stress (long-term, systemic). It should outline the physiological mechanism (cortisol and adrenaline release) and common triggers like workplace burnout or life transitions.

How to deal:

Completing the stress cycle: Physical activity—even a brisk 15-minute walk or shaking out your limbs—metabolizes built-up stress hormones and signals to your body that the "threat" has passed.

Boundary setting: Learning to say no and establishing hard cut-offs for work or draining obligations to prevent chronic build-up.

Prioritization triage: Using frameworks like the Eisenhower Matrix to separate truly urgent tasks from those that are just noisy and overwhelming.
